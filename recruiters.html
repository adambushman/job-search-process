Finding

Recruiters


Theory

Recruiters specialize in finding candidates to fill jobs for companies. There's two types of recruiters, generally:

  • Headhunter (3rd party)
    • They get paid to find the client (the company) a candidates who ultimately gets the job
    • They generally have a great pulse on the industry as a whole but won't have all the details on the job
  • Corporate recruiter
    • Their full-time job with the company is to find the best candidates for every position
    • They'll be intimately familiar with the job in question though may not have as helpful a perspective on the industry as a whole

In summary: recruiters serve their client or employeer (the company)


Practice

If you're approached by a recruiter through social media or a job site, don't hesitate to engage with them. At the very least, it's a networking opportunity for you to land in their archives for potential opportunities in the future.

Get a sense for what type of recruiter they are and why they feel you and the company are a good match.

Consider soliciting them for any feedback on your candidacy relative to the job and or industry.


Application

  1. Recruiters are a component companies use to find candidates; see them as an asset who's ultimately an ally of the company
  2. Engage with recruiters as an investment that, were it to ever pay off, will likely be in the future
