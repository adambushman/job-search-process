Finding

Where


Theory

One of the principles for finding your next opportunity is to cast a wide net. Often, candidates pigeon hole themselvles into too narrow of a focus (only job X, only via site Y, only looking Z days of the week). We want to get away from that!

Specifically as it applies to "where", your most traditional approach will be with job sites. The most popular being:

  • Indeed
  • LinkedIn
  • ZipRecruiter
  • Glassdoor

How it works is these job sites charge employers for listing their jobs. Naturally, because there's a fee and these big hiring platforms boast so many candidates, they'll generally stick to one or two sites. That means, 1) no job exists everywhere and 2) your best avenue for a wide net is multiple sites, especially since its free to the applicant.

For my last job search, I relied heavily on Indeed but ultimately found my current opportunity thanks to LinkedIn.

In contrasting terms, per Zipdo 80% of available jobs are not publicly advertised. So where are they?

They exist in people's brains and private conversations. You get in on those by developing a network you actively engage. The best time to build up this network is when you don't have to.

In summary: cast a wide net by following available jobs in the market from different sites.


Practice

Create an account with as many of the top job sites as you feel comfortable with. Sign up for email/text notifications. Consider getting their app on your phone so you can substitute down time you'd use for mobile gaming with applying for jobs.

You may or may not have built up a network in you industry. If not, start now for your next search. If you do, leverage them. Put out feelers, make your intentions known, etc.


Application

  1. Setup and configure your accounts at major job sites
  2. Develop a network now or leverage your existing one
