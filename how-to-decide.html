Who/What

How to decide


This section isn't for everyone

If the previous sections have clarified which jobs and industries to pursue, skip this section. If the previous sections left you questioning which jobs and industries to pursue, continue with this section.

Theory

Think back to your childhood. Did you parents insist or support you participating in some extra-curricular? Mine certainly did. They believed in well-rounded individuals so I was exposed to sports, the arts, local government, etc.

It became abundantly clear what I liked and didn't like as I gained exposure. I wasn't a fan of soccer but loved basketball. Dance was fine but musical theater really resonated.

We all may have a theory or belief about what we'll enjoy, but that's cultivated with trial and error, not some innate sense. We generate the hieuristic (mental shortcut), we aren't exclusively born with it.

To discover which jobs and industries to pursue, you'll need to do some research and be exposed to the gammut. Through trial and error, you'll start to narrow in on your field.

In summary: get exposure to what's out there, eliminating options and finding your calling.


Practice

Remember in the "Start with why" section we outlined motivations for changing jobs? These are the exact items for which to research industries, companies, and jobs.

I've found success and recommend people use the following resources and techniques for research:

  • "Day in the life of a..." and "What skills does a...need?" videos on YouTube; it's a quick way to eliminate misaligned professions
  • Payscale and Glassdoor give insight into compensation by company, industry, and even job title
  • LinkedIn gives insight into specific companies and professionals in your space, including context to what might be expected
  • Indeed and ZipRecruiter gives insight into what jobs are out there and what companies want out of their prospective employees

As you work throught these and other materials, continue to ask yourself: 1) Am I comfortable with industry expectations? 2) Does what I'm finding align with my who, what, how?


Application

  1. Research industries, companies, jobs, and professionals; this information will push and pull you into a sense for your calling.
