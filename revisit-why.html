Offer

Revisit your why


Theory

You've got an offer! This is amazing news and a big payoff for all your hard work. It should also be validation of everything you outlined in the process!

It's a good time to reflect back on the start of the journey: why you wanted a change in the first place?

While amazing you received an offer, don't misinterpret the next step: evaluation. You must critique this offer and judge it against the benchmarks you set out for (or modified along the way per feedback from the market).

Ask yourself: does this offer align with my why?

In summary: don't blindly accept the offer, as flattering as it may seem.


Practice

Dig up the first documents you ever created: 1) your why/timeline and 2) your "must haves"/"nice to haves". Review these personally, then with those close to you whom you trust (spouse, friend, mentor).

Be honest with yourself on if the offer aligns with your personal benchmarks. It can be difficult, but don't be afraid to decline the offer and continue your search.

I received an offer during my seach that hit some of the items in both categories, but didn't address all of my "must haves". Furthermore, it didn't align enough with my why.

As excited and validated as I felt to receive the offer, some hours later I declined and continued my search. I held out for better alignment and when it arrived, it was blissful.


Application

  1. Question and critique the offer, judging it against your personal benchmarks.
  2. Don't be afraid to decline and continue your seach for better alignment (you didn't do all this work to settle)
