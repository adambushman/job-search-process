Marketing Tools

Assets


Theory

As great as one's message may be, the inability to share it could cripple the mission. The delivery system to the target audience is critial and that's what we're after in a job search.

We'll create some assets that take our wonderful content forward, compelling prospective employers to get us in for a sitdown.

In summary: great messages necessitate great delivery systems to change the world.


Practice

The message you're sharing is the value you can bring to prospective employers. That message will reach their ears in a variety of ways. We'll create a specific asset that best aligns with the natural pros and cons of each.

Resume

A resume is the classic content delivery system. But just because it's tried and true doesn't mean you can't maximize its potential. Here's some tips:

In case it wasn't clear, all the content you've created should feed these assets. You're just fitting it for purpose. If you've been exhaustive and specific enough thus far, assembling the resume should be pretty stress free.

  • Customize the resume to the job search; you don't need a different resume per opportunity you apply for
  • Resumes should be easily digestible; that means readable in ~3 mins and a single, one-sided page
  • Focus on impact and value to be created; it should be true but framed in an attractive manner
  • Design something visually stunning; most resumes feature a boring format so this is an area to stand out.
  • Format and compartamentalize your document for easy reference and digestion

Check out these examples. If you were the hiring manager, which would you remember from a stack of 50?


Cover Letter

A cover letter, if you're unfamiliar, is a way to address an individual opportunity and stress key points of alignment. Where the resume is the wide net to cast, the cover letter adds a personal touch.

Most companies don't require a cover letter therefore most applicants never submit one. Sounds like an opportunity for you to stick out from the crowd!

  • Customize the cover letter to each application; address the company by name (even the hiring manager, if known)
  • Highlight with specifics why you and the company are a good pair
  • However you've styled your resume, use the same visuals to reinforce your distinction from the masses
  • Close with a professional salutation and signature

Portfolio

A portfolio is a compliation of one's detailed work that can be shared publicly. In certain industries, portfolios are mandatory so be sure you're aware of expectations.

Should it be an optional or even rare expectation in your industry, there's a temptation to circumvent this asset. But remember two things:

  1. 1. The toughest part for employers in the process is to reconcile what little is known about a candidate with what they expect of the applicant once hired; a portfolio shows you can do the job
  2. 2. You're aiming for the road less traveled because job searches are a numbers game. That's how you get noticed!

Let's get into what your portfolio should feature.

  • The key is that you're demonstrating projects relevant to the job with great detail about process and results
  • DO something, SHOW it, and HIGHLIGHT the value created
  • Can either be a document (physical or digital) or a website
  • If you plan to include a project from past employment, seek permission to share publicly like LinkedIn (if that's okay, anything is fair game)
  • Make sure any stying matches your other assets

Video Intro

Most all job application mediums are digital these days so you have opportunities most never did and that many still don't take advantage of. One of those is an area to "upload additional files".

This is usually where your cover letter and digital portfolio will be submitted but there's no rule against other files. A great option is an introductory video of yourself.

We've talked about it frequently now, but one big risk for employers is not knowing too little about a candidate which raises their opportunity cost. You can help dispell that risk by giving them insight into your personality, communication style, etc.

  • Ensure your vieo is professional (a solid color backdrop, industry appropriate clothing, etc)
  • Make sure it gives a good impression (clear audio, crisp video, and proper lighting)
  • It should be short (1-2 mins) and to the point; leverage your summaries
