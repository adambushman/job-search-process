Distribution


Theory

We've said it before, but the road less traveled absolutely tells you something. It literal terms, it could indicate the safety or efficiency of the traveling route.

In figurative terms, it's an opportunity to distinguish yourself from the masses. That's the exact route we want to take given the competitive landscape.

Per Constant Contact, only 7% of job seekers have a personal website, yet 56% of emplyers are more impressed by such thann any other aspect of a candidate's profile.

Take the road less traveled.

Per Zipdo, including a cover letter with a job application increases your changes of getting hired by 40%.

Take the road less traveled.

Per TopResume 54% of employers have rejected candidates based off of social media. That means a good portion of the remaining 46% candidate profiles made no positive impression and the small remaining share contributed positively to the hiring process.

Take the road less traveled.

In summary: when flooding the market with your assets, lean into the areas of opportunity.


Practice

The primary mechanism for distributing your assets will be in the form of a file or a link. Job applications will provide areas for upload and pasting each.

You'll want to ensure your documents are PDFs, sized appropriately for the web, and that links are active and accessible.

Most application sites will also invite you to complete a "digital resume/profile". This may seem like excess work, but by filling in their forms, your search will be supercharged.

  • Candidates will be referred to jobs that align with education, experience, and skills expectations; a lot of the work pre-done
  • Employers get a detailed view of potential candidates with whom they align and will invite you personally to apply to their job
  • Personalized notifications will ensure you retain a pulse on the job market
  • Allows for quick application since at minimum you share what's already on file (which aligns with your existing assets)
  • You should complete this step for every job site you leverage in your search

If you still need more convincing, there's a saying about taking some sort of path others often forego...
