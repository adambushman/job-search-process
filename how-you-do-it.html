Who/What

How you do it


Theory

It's time to connect the dots! We addressed who you are and what you do, let's now consider the how. How do you go about your what that does/doesn't align with who you are?

For most employers, the how of it all is the most important. Take two nurses who's list of "whats" is virtually identical. They're likely differentiated in their how:

  • Perhaps one is surly where the other is kind
  • Perhaps one is organized where the other is scatter brained
  • Perhaps one connects with patients while the other seems aloof
  • Perhaps one innovates while the other sticks with what works

In summary: you will more than likely differentiate yourself in the job process by showcasing your how, not belaboring your what.


Practice

Gather your notes on "who you are" and "what you do/would like to do". Start attaching adjectives or descriptors to each what that aligns with your who.

Make sure that these uniquely express you. For example, shy away from "work hard" (who doesn't feel they work hard). Instead, consider "quality focused" or "driven by the details". Keep it authentic, but highlight what makes you different.


Application

  1. Create an intersection of your "who" and "what" by describing how you go about your work in a unique way.
