Interviews

Interpersonal


Theory

The traditional, interpersonal interview, has a handful of purposes. Most notably, employers get to assess how many intangiles stack up to their requirements. These include intelligence, communication, personality, etc.

You could have the greatest assets, which landed you interviews in the first place, but you won't be ushered along the funnel without presenting your content in a way that sells yourself as an employee they must have.

In summary: sell yourself as an employee they need and make them sell their opportunity to you.


Practice

Let's run through some principles of the interpersonal interview:

  • Have your summaries down pat; these will keep you focused and help weave the details of who, what, how with the value you can provide
  • Be flexible; read the room, see the direction, and tailor your content to align
  • Be personable, friendly; they are nervous too so smiles, gratitude, and measured confidence go a long way with impressions
  • Come with your own questions! It's a bad look when you don't and its not in the spirit of "sell me your opportunity". Questions like the below are great:
    • What makes your company stand out in this industry as a place for professionals to do their best work?
    • In what ways can this role push the company to achieving its short and long term goals?
    • What might someone successful in this role over the next 5 years come to expect for advancing in the company?
  • Remember, this is the time to confirm this opportunity is worth your time; address any lingering doubts from you list of "must haves" and "nice to haves"
