Why

Timeline


Theory

Time often dictates what is possible. Assume you're running 10 minutes late for an appointment 20 minutes away. There's no conceivable way to sufficiently speed and make up the time.

In a similar vein, considering your deadline for landing your next opportunity will dictate what's possible.

  • Suppose you're out of work with minimal savings. You'll likely need relax your criteria and use existing resources. Perhaps, you may need to consider a gap job.
  • Say you're currently in school with two (2) semesters left until graduation. You have a lot of time to follow the process correctly and achieve great results.

In summary: consider that what's possible in your job search may primarily be a function of time.


Practice

Review your circumstances and outline when you'll be seizing the opportunity. Talk with those closest to and evaluate the resources (money, commitments, etc.) that could help or hurt your timeline.

Work to put your timeline into numbers (ie 6) and units (ie months). It may also be worthwhile to think in a range (ie 4-6 months).


Application

  1. Be honest with yourself about when you look to land that opportunity; put it into words on paper.
