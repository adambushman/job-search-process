Marketing Tools

Branding


Theory

Branding is all about owning property within one's brain. We see this all the time.

The silouhette of a bitten fruit reminds us of Apple, red polo + khaki pants brings to mind State Farm, and ice cream sandwiches are often coined as Fat Boys, no matter the brand.

In summary: use visual elements to tell a story and instill a feeling employers can't help but always associate with you.


Practice

Branding encompasses a lot. It's basically how you represent yourself virtually and is reinforced when face-to-face with your audience.

You'll want to align branding to the industry, job title, and your own personality. Don't sacrifice legibility, understanding, etc., to chase a brand. Don't misrepresent, either. It only leads to confusion and red flags in the hiring process.

Branding will include, but isn't limited, to:

  • Tone
  • Message
  • Vocabulatory
  • Font
  • Colors
  • Design

Leverage branding to tie together all your content spread throughout your assets, distinguishing yourself amongs the crowd.

Alex "The Analyst" is a big personality in the data industry. He's achiving consistent branding in his LinkedIn, YouTube, and other mediums he employs.


Application

  1. Think about the feeling and perception you want others to have when interacting with you and your assets
  2. Work to capture that in each of the areas listed above; inject those elements into your assets
