Offer

Beware the first


Theory

Your first offer is special! It's validation of all your hard work and it's an exceptional feeling to know you're wanted. Additionally, it's the ultimate feeling of control over your career and future.

But that's precisely why you need to be EXTRA cautious of your first offer. This is when you're most vulnerable!

You've been on one heck of a journey and you'd love nothing more than to end with a new opportunity. Perhaps, you even feel a need to pounce on the opportunity. These and other biases are exactly why we outlined your why, what, and how so clearly. It's why we spent so much time on your "must haves"/"nice to haves".

Trust in your past self that filled these out. Don't blindly accept the first offer. If you do end up accepting the first, make sure you're objectively aligning with those benchmarks.


In summary: Check yourself with the first offer and see the situation for what it is: a potential solution at your most vulnerable


Practice

Surround yourself with people who can help you objectively weight the offer against your benchmarks. Make sure you trust these individuals and listen to their counsel.

Take your time; reflect on the offer in the context of all you set out to accomplish.


Application

  1. Once you reject an offer, you're less predisposed to biases affecting your decision making
  2. If you do accept the first offer, just be sure you're objectively aligning with the objective you set out for
