Who/What

What you do/don't do


Theory

Phew! Now that you've done the hard part, you can now do the easier part.

First off, what do you do? To be clear, I don't mean job title or profession (see how we keep disassociating from that traditional concept). What I mean is what are the things you do in your job that creates value for the company?

You may be tempted to just write a list of tasks but what we're after is the essence of what you do, not the mundane details that are necessary with any profession.

Tangentially, consider what you 1) DON'T DO, 2) WOULD LIKE TO DO, and 3) WOULD NOT LIKE TO DO. With these other categories you start to zero in on how you view your future profession.

In summary: start looking at what you do as value activities, not job title or industry.


Practice

Review your current and past recent employment. Start with job title and description if you must but push past that to the value you create for the company.

  • Sell product
  • Cultivate relationships
  • Clarify compay message
  • Simplify the complex with clear process
  • Get people on the same page
  • Track the status of big projects and related tasks
  • Remove obstacles from the way of executors
  • Create designs that resonate with our consumers
  • Transform the abstract into the tangible
  • Leverage technology around me for efficiency

Now, use the same process to fill the other sections: what you don't do, would like to do, wouldn't like to do. Don't feel the need to be as exhaustive with the first and last, just enough to eliminate types of work and value that doesn't align with who you are.

Consider questions like the following:

  • What kind of problems do you enjoy solving?
  • What do you hate being assigned?
  • What drains your drive?
  • What invites your mind to think "out-of-the-box"?

Application

  1. Create a short list in each of 4 categories that detail the what of your job in terms of value created for the company.
    • What you do (be exhaustive)
    • What don't you do (be supperficial)
    • What you would like to do (be exhaustive)
    • What you would not like to do (be supperficial)
