Check-in #1

Content Alignment


Theory

Messages to an audience resonate when the talking points are connected and consistent. When the train of thought is unclear or the narration features too many detours, the audience is left confused about what to take away.

You want your message, in any form it may take, to align and support each other. Every detail necessary, every story relevant, and every component building to the key message.

In summary: you'll resonate with employers when they're left with a clear vision of the candidate.


Practice

To this point, you've outlined A LOT of content. Let's review, quickly, the documents you've filled in:

  • "WHY" - Summary of your motivations
  • "WHO" - A description of the person and professional you are
  • "WHAT" - A description of what you do, don't do, and aspirations
  • "HOW" - A description of how you do work differently from others
  • "INTRODUCTORY DETAILS" - A list of traditional items for a resume
  • "HIGHLIGHTS" - Your best bits that uniquely frame you career from another
  • "SUMMARIES" - Consise statements and stories that provide an overview of your content

Everything you've done needs to be consistent and aligned. Review it all and look for any sharp edges or irrelevant pieces. Consult with those closest to you for feedback. Additionally, if you know someone within the industry you're interested in, seek their advice.

Up next we will transpose this content into marketing tools, or mediums employers will be exposed to you (applications, resume, etc). We'll have plenty to worry about there to waste brain power fixing incongrument material.


Application

  1. Invest the time to ensure your content is aligned and will resonate with employers, no matter the delivery system.
