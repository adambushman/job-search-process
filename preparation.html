Interviews

Preparation


Theory

Interviews are the prize of all the assets you generated. You got the interview because you stood out from the masses with the design and content of your resume, cover letter, portfolio, etc.

Entering the interview presents a different challenge. Regurgitating what's in those documents will leave prospective employers with insufficient detail to evaluate your candidacy. This is because they want to hear more about your who, why, what, how and less about your introductory details.

Additionally, you have the opportunity to interview the company. Just as they are evaluating you, you must make your own assessment of them. This is a mutual courtship!

In Summary: your approach to interviews will be different than what's thought of traditionally. You need to be prepared!


Practice

Let's dig into each interview type specifically and what you should do to prepare:


Phone interview
  • These usually come out of the blue; you won't have any warning so the prep time has to come RIGHT AFTER you apply
  • Document your application and add some context notes:
    • What's the company industry, size, reputation?
    • What items from your "motivations" list aren't clearly addressed in the listing?
  • Keep this info handy (perhaps on your phone) so when your phone rings you are ready to make a good first impression
  • Come prepared with questions to fill any gaps in the job listing
  • Don't waste your time with more interviews if answers from this one aren't meeting your "must haves"

Interpersonal interview
  • These are scheduled ahead of time so you will have plenty of time to do more indepth research
  • That research should include
    • Review of public articles, websites, social media, etc.
    • Lookup the interview personnel via LinkedIn, their backgrounds and skills
  • Use this research to identify specific examples of problems you can solve with this specific company
  • You want to have prepared questions to ask, specifically questions that address your motivations list:
    • What is the company's perspective on work life balance? What mechanisms are in place to foster it?
    • What opportunities for advancement are available in this position long term?
    • What are some specific challenges this position could help resolve in the first 3 months?

Technical interview
  • Not every job or industry necessitates a technical interview where you demonstrate your skills but if they do you will know ahead of time
  • Feel free to ask what's the nature of the question or subject matter it covers
  • Ask for clear expectations, like:
    • Will I have use of the internet?
    • Is the interview timed?
  • Ultimately, however, your preparation for this aspect of the interview needs to have happened weeks and months before interview time

Final interview
  • If you've made it this far, you're likely trying to best 2-3 other candidates
  • Come prepared with ways to reinforce value:
    • Perhaps bring a suggested action plan to address a specific need
    • Maybe bring materials from a recent project to highlight in the interview
  • Make sure that any preparation starts and ends with something valuable provided that you bring uniquely

Application

  1. Preparation is about tailoring your content to the circumstances of each interview
  2. Don't wait until the last minute to prepare
