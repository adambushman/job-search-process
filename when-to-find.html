Finding

When


Theory

In short, always. In sales they often say, "always be closing". Well, here, you should always be finding.

Think about the natural flow of things. You find 10 jobs, start applying. You hear back on two job for phone interviews and that's all that becomes of it. If you stopped applying at the 10 jobs, you're now at square one.

This game, job hunting, is a volume exercise. There's various statistics out there but here's two interesting ones:

  • Per Zipdo, the median job posting receives 250 resumes
  • Per Zipdo, only 2.5% (or 2-4 candidates) make it to the final interview

In summary: don't feel satisfied with your work to-date; keep working the process


Practice

You want to stagger your jobs, ideally having 20-30 applications "submitted" and a handful in various stages of "interviews"...all at the same time! This ensures youre pipeline is full. You can always turn off the faucet if you accept an offer.
